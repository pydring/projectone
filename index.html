The Beginning
The Pilgrims landed in New England to find a new land free of religious persecution that was free for the taking.
Native Friends
The Pilgrims met Squanto, the Native American who helped them make friends with the Patuxet tribe.
His name is actually Tisquantum and was kidnapped when he was young and sold into slavery in Europe. There he learned English.
When he eventually escaped and returned he found his village had been destroyed by disease.
The Harsh Winter
The Pilgrims quietly suffered through their first harsh Winter with some assistance from their new native friends.
The Pilgrims actually pillaged Native American burial sites and graves and looted homes with the intention of "paying them back" but they "forgot to" but the tribes still helped them
Thanksgiving Time
The Pilgrims and Native Americans sat down to a feast after their first successful harvest together and remained friends.
Most of this is true:
They did have a feast.
They did remain friends until more settlers came to America.
Be Aware of the Good and the Bad of History.
The only reason the land was free for the taking was that diseases from European fishermen killed off 90% of the Native Americans in New England.
